AI Tutor
Context: Lecture 06
Explain DNS simply. Why does it matter?
DNS is the internet’s “address book.” Humans remember names (like example.com) but networks route using IP addresses. DNS translates names → IPs so your device knows where to send packets.
Why it matters: it’s a single lookup that can affect speed, privacy, and security. If DNS is slow or compromised, everything downstream suffers.
